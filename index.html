OUR SPEAKERS

DR.SANTHAKUMAR MOHAN

PhD IN ROBOTICS AND CONTROL

Santhakumar Mohan got his Ph.D. (Robotics and Control) from the Indian Institute of Technology Madras, Chennai (India) in 2010. From June 2010 to March 2011, he worked as an assistant professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the National Institute of Technology Calicut (NITC), Kerala (India).Currently, he is serving as Technical Editor, of IEEE/ASME Transactions on Mechatronics (2020-present), Associate Editor, of IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters (2020-2024), Advances in Mechanical Engineering (2023-present)

Dr. A. P. Sudheer

Associate Professor, Mechatronics/Robotics lab in Charge, Mechanical Engineering, NIT Calicut

Dr. Sudheer's research interests primarily focus on robotics, with significant contributions to areas such as kinematic and dynamic analysis of spatial parallel manipulators and EEG-based emotion recognition. In addition to his teaching and research responsibilities, he has served as the Associate Dean (Planning & Development) at NIT Calicut

SAARANG SUMESH

CHILD PROGIDY

A child prodigy from Kochi who has invented many robots, including a cleaning robot, a walking stick for the visually impaired, and a smart car seat belt He was the youngest TEDx speaker in the world His father is an electrical engineer who works in the United States

Abhishek Kaushik

Assistant Professor at digital university (tvm)

Abhishek Kaushik is an Assistant Professor at the School of Computer Science and Engineering, Digital University Kerala, Trivandrum, India. His research interests include Computer Vision, Robotics, SLAM, AI/ML, and Medical Image Processing. He previously worked as a Post-Doctoral Research Associate at the University of Edinburgh, UK, and ACTREC, TMC, Mumbai. He has also served as an Assistant Professor at Madhav Institute of Technology and Sciences, Gwalior. Additionally, he worked as a Project Assistant at DRDE, Gwalior. His expertise spans advanced AI-driven robotics and medical imaging applications.

diwakar vaish

Scientist

Diwakar Vaish is an Indian robotics researcher and the Head of Robotics and Research at A-SET Training and Research Institutes. He developed India's first 3D-printed humanoid robot, Manav, and the world's first production brain-controlled wheelchair. Vaish also co-created the world's smallest and cheapest ventilator in collaboration with AIIMS Delhi. His innovative work in robotics and AI makes him an engaging and insightful speaker for Droid 5.0.
